The Hackathon

Hackathon is an initiative by Computer society of India at GLBITM for students of different colleges to show case their talent and compete in a professional world by working on real time problems and innovate some great solutions to them. Be ready to grab this golden opportunity to show us your technical and professional skills.

9:00 A.M., 16th February, 2019

Code of Conduct

₹50.00/- per head

Hackathon is a theme-based, 24-hour, development hackathon.Teams (with a number of 4 to 6 members each) will be given three themes to choose from, which will be declared on the day of the event.

  1. The teams, in a duration of four hours, will have to submit a solution/plan/roadmap of what they plan to implement and its effect. One phase of scoring will be conducted for this.
  2. Then, the teams will develop their ideas in full swing. Along with this, one question will be uploaded every two hours on an online platform. This will provide an opportunity for the teams to score Hackos points.
  3. The last two hours will be dedicated purely to the presentation of the solution developed and the solutions will be analysed. This will be the final and most weighted scoring. The total score will be calculated, and the winner will be announced.
  • The participants will be provided complimentary lunch and dinner, along with breakfast coupons
  • To be held in G. L. Bajaj Institute of Technology & Management, Plot No. 2, Knowledge Park III, Greater Noida
  • Reporting time is 3:00Pm. on October 11, 2018

  • The minimum number of participants in a team can be 4 and upto 6. There is no lower limit on the number of team members allowed.
  • Every member of the team must be an undergraduate student.
  • The competition will proceed with random broad themes on the day of the hackathon. The teams will be given freedom to choose any one of those themes.
  • Teams will be asked to propose their idea in the first four hours of the hackathon in the form of an illustrative text.
  • The next step of the hackathon will be the actual development of the product idea proposed by the teams. In this step, 15+ hours will be provided to develop the code required and prepare a presentation.
  • Each team will be required to make a commit of their progress on the HackerEarth ID provided by the team every two hours.
  • Copying segments of code/or entire code from previous projects is NOT allowed.
  • You can use resources such as Stack Overflow and GitHub. In general, internet resources are allowed. However, direct plagiarism of ideas/code is not allowed.
  • There is no restriction on the programming languages and platforms used to develop the product.
  • In between the product development phase, some competitive coding questions will be floated every 2 hours for the participants. Solving these will earn you Hackos points (B).
  • The final stage of the hackathon will be conclusive with a 3-minute presentation of the developed prototype.
  • Girls are allowed to stay at night if and only if they have written permission from their parents.
  • Keep your paytm payment screenshots with you.
You must agree with the Code of Conduct before participating in the Hackathon.

Judging will be done in this final stage based on the following criteria, with equal weightage for the constituting sub-points:

  1. Proposal(P)
    1. Innovation
    2. Uniqueness of the proposal
    3. Importance of the problem solved
    4. Practicability of the proposal
  2. Final Presentation(FP)
    1. Presentation Skills
    2. Quality of the product
    3. Percentage of original proposal attained
    4. Scalability
    • You can use HackerEarth to submit your deliverables.
    • The total marks will be calculated from all the above factors as follows:
    • Total Marks: 50(P) + 40(FP) + 10(B) = 100
    • The judges of the competition will evaluate the teams on the above-mentioned criteria.
    • Based on these evaluation criteria, the team with maximum marks will be the winner.

    The judges’ decision will be final.

    All participants are requested to produce their college ID cards at the time of the event. No other identity proof will be accepted as we need the evidence of you being a part of an undergraduate course.
